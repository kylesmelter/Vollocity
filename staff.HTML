Kelsey Brandl

To say Kelsey knows the sport would be an understatement. She began her volleyball career at the age of nine playing for a club team at Minnesota Nothern Lights. After two years she joined a club team at Minnesota One (M1) Volleyball. It was during these years she laid the foundation for one of the most successful prep volleyball careers in the state of Minnesota. Playing for Lakeville North, she lettered all four years as a middle-hitter, and led her to team to a school-record 30 wins (30-2) during her senior season, and topped the year off with a third-place finish in the 3A State Finals. Her prep play earned her a slew of accolades over the years, including:

  • 2007 Tribune Metro Player of the Year
  • Ranked the No. 25 senior in the country according to prepvolleyball.com
  • Three-time all-conference and all-state selection
  • Member of the U.S. Girls' Youth National Team
After gradauting from high school, she went on to play one year at the University of Cincinnati before transferring and finishing her college career playing three years at Ball State University in Indiana. During her time at Ball State, she earned First-team All-MAC honors each of her three years, and still ranks third in program history with a .340 career hitting percentage. Outside of her career as a player, she also has a wealth of experience in coaching. The breadth of her coaching spans the spectrum—including developmetals in players age 10 and younger all the way up to collegiate level, including stints at St. Olaf College and Hamline University as an assistant coach. But when she's not on the court, her passion for educating extends beyond the sport and into her career as a nurse and clinical resource specialist. She also enjoys spending time outdoors and hiking the endless amount of trails the Pacific Northwest has to offer, as well as hanging out with her two cats, Sierra and Sequoia.
